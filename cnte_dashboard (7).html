Current Situation
  • CNTE marched toward Estadio Azteca on June 11 but voluntarily withdrew without clashes when they encountered SSC police lines. World Cup opened without major protest disruption.
  • Plantón in Centro Histórico remains in place — now in its 11th day. Blockades on Reforma/Circuito Interior corridor continuing.
  • AICM flights operational throughout. No confirmed AICM access disruption at any point.
Key Changes
  • Fourth Segob negotiation round on June 11 ran for six hours — partial agreements reached but described by CNTE as insufficient. First time "agreements" has been used in this cycle.
  • CNTE explicitly instructed members to avoid confrontation on June 11 — no flags on poles, peaceful withdrawal. Represents a tactical de-escalation.
  • Strike continues and plantón remains — CNTE's National Assembly confirmed no suspension pending further negotiation.
Key Implications
  • The June 11 World Cup pressure window has passed without full materialisation — overall risk downgrades from Severe to High.
  • Staff on Reforma/Circuito Interior still face daily disruption — plantón and strike remain active. Journey management stays in effect.
  • Partial agreements in Segob talks are the first genuine de-escalation signal — monitor for CNTE assembly decision on whether to accept.
