Current Situation
  • Plantón in Centro Histórico now in its 11th day. Blockades on Reforma and Circuito Interior continuing — no geographic expansion but sustained daily disruption.
  • On June 11, clashes erupted near Estadio Azteca — Bloque Negro breached Gate 8, attacked police with stones and petards. 7 detained, 6 police injured, fires reported, vehicle overturned. CNTE leadership had instructed peaceful conduct; Bloque Negro appears to be a distinct group.
  • AICM flights operational throughout. Heightened security at terminals deterred confirmed access blockade.
Key Changes
  • June 11 clashes near the stadium — 7 detained, 6 police injured, fires and an overturned vehicle — represent the most significant violence incident of this cycle.
  • Fourth Segob round on June 11 produced partial agreements — first in this cycle — but CNTE described them as insufficient. Strike and plantón remain in place.
  • Sheinbaum acknowledged the Zócalo fan fest may not be usable, offering 18 alternative venues — protest movement is now directly affecting World Cup operations.
Key Implications
  • CBD travel remains possible but unpredictable — Reforma and Circuito Interior subject to daily blockades. Use Periférico or northern routes to avoid protest corridors. Allow significant buffer time; remote working recommended for Centro Histórico-based staff.
  • AICM operational but access risk persists — allow 2–3 hour buffers for airport transfers. Monitor SSC CDMX and AICM channels before travel.
  • Avoid active protest flashpoints — June 11 clashes confirm real safety risk near marches, stadium corridors, and Zócalo perimeter.
