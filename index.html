Key Insights
Current Situation
  • Fifth consecutive day of blockades on Circuito Interior and Reforma–Chapultepec, directly impairing Torre Virreyes access. ~10,000 businesses in Centro Histórico affected.
  • Violence remains organised and contained to government sites — no spread to commercial zones. No major new incidents June 4–5.
  • AICM flights operational. Highway toll-booth occupations ongoing but with free passage permitted.
Key Changes
  • Government tabled a new pension proposal on June 4 (public insurer for ISSSTE individual accounts) — CNTE called a national assembly to review it but rejected it as insufficient.
  • Sheinbaum has ruled out a direct meeting; three rounds of Gobernación talks have ended without agreement.
  • Highway casetas released after June 4 actions — a minor de-escalation signal, but plantón and strike remain firmly in place.
Key Implications
  • Six days to June 11 — CNTE has a concrete government proposal on the table but core demands (ISSSTE repeal, 100% salary rise) remain unmet.
  • Staff on Reforma/Circuito Interior face continued daily disruption with no normalisation expected before the World Cup.
  • If the national assembly rejects the proposal outright, escalation ahead of June 11 becomes the base case.
